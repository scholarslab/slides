Key Sizes, Revisited

Schneier, Applied Cryptography: There's a limit to brute-forcing large key sizes that "have nothing to do with the technology of the devices; they are the maximums that thermodynamics will allow. And they strongly imply that brute-force attacks against 256-bit keys will be infeasible until computers are built from something other than matter and occupy something other than space."
